## Brief Overview of OctoAcme Project Management Processes
OctoAcme follows a lightweight stage-gate approach that emphasizes delivering customer value through iterative increments and data-informed decisions. Projects progress through initiation (one-pager and stakeholder alignment), planning (prioritized backlog, Definition of Done, risk register), execution (timeboxed sprints, small pull requests, CI-driven testing), release (pre-release checks, staged deploys, rollback plans), and close (retrospectives and action items).

Clear roles guide accountability: Product Managers define outcomes and prioritize work; Project Managers coordinate delivery, schedules, risks, and stakeholder communication; Developers build and test; QA validates acceptance criteria. Communication is driven by a regular cadence — daily standups, weekly delivery syncs, sprint demos, and monthly stakeholder updates — and an escalation path for blockers.

Quality assurance is embedded through unit, integration, and smoke tests, security scanning in CI, manual QA when required, and release gates that require passing CI, documented release notes, and rollback plans. Teams track velocity and success metrics defined in the project one-pager and use dashboards to monitor operational signals and inform post-release retrospectives.
